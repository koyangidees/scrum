What is SCRUM?

Scrum is an iterative and incremental framework for project management mainly deployed in agile software development. The scrum methodology emphasizes functional software, the flexibility to change along with emerging business realities, communication and collaboration.

A Glossary of Scrum

  • Scrum: Framework for team collaboration on complex projects
  • Artifact: Provides key information that the Scrum Team and the stakeholders need to be aware of for understanding the product under development, the activities done, and the activities being planned in the project. (Product Backlog, Sprint Backlog, Burn-Down Chart, Increment)
  • Product Backlog: Work that need to be done in specific order.
  • Backlog Item(PBI): A unit of work small enough to be completed by a team in one Sprint iteration.
  • Burn-down Chart: How much effort is left compared to time.
  • Daily Scrum: A 15-minute time-boxed event for the Development Team and is held every day of the Sprint.
  • Product Owner: The manager who is responsible for product and team.
  • Scrum Board: A board that visualises progress for team.
  • Scrum Master: Scrum master's role is like a coach who helps team with their know-how.
  • Sprint: Short tasks. One following right after the completion of another
  • Sprint Backlog: What the team needs to complete the sprint.
